Oak Valley Coding Tournament

Welcome to OVCT, the Oak Valley Coding Tournament! This tournament is created by our team at Oak Valley Coding Club. There are two divisions, the Group A Division and the Group B/C Division. The Group A Division contains problems that uses concepts from the USACO Silver Division, while the Group B/C Division uses concepts from the USACO Bronze Division.

1. This contest is represented by the Oak Valley Coding Club, a middle school club in Oak Valley Middle school, San Diego, CA. This contest is in no way affiliated with USACO, CF, CSES, or any other competitive programming affiliates

2. To compete in this contest, you must follow all of the following conditions: - You are a pre-collegiate student - You are in USACO Silver or below (i.e: Sorry, but no USACO Gold/Plat contestants allowed) - You have a CF rating of <1700 (Basically, you are at a USACO Bronze - Silver level)

3. There are two divisions: Novice and Intermediate. The difficulty of Novice is equivalent to USACO Bronze, and Intermediate is roughly equivalent to USACO Silver. If you are in USACO Bronze you may compete in either Novice or Intermediate, but if you are in USACO Silver you must compete in Intermediate.

4. The prizes will be as follows: Intermediate: 1st: $50, 2nd: $40, 3rd: $30, 4th: $20, 5th: $10 Novice: 1st: $30, 2nd: $20, 3rd: $10

5. Java, Python, C++ are the allowed languages. Java has a time limit of 4 seconds, Python 10 seconds, C++ 2 seconds.

6. This contest takes place on Hackerrank. You will need a Hackerrank ID.

7. During the contest, you may only access the respective documentations of your language (i.e: java docs). You may not access programming guides like USACO Guide or others

8. These rules are subject to change at all times, and senior staff are granted objective reviewal over certain cases.

Contact using our discord server for the OVCT Competition: Discord Server
